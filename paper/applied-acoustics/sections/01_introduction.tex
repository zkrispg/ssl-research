\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Sound event localization and detection (SELD) is the array-acoustics problem of
jointly estimating \emph{what} sound events are active and \emph{where} they
originate---the latter a direction-of-arrival (DOA) estimation problem on a
microphone array---and it underpins far-field automatic speech recognition,
robot audition, augmented reality, and acoustic
monitoring~\cite{adavanne2018seld,politis2020overview}. SELD systems consume
multichannel audio delivered either as a compact microphone array (MIC) or as
first-order Ambisonics (FOA), and a recurring question in array signal
processing is how much \emph{array geometry} the network should be told
explicitly. Many methods inject geometry through attention biases, positional
encodings, or geometry-derived features~\cite{kowalk2022geometry}, on the
intuition that more spatial prior helps; a geometry-invariant line argues instead
that inter-channel phase already suffices~\cite{baek2025gidoaenet,neuralsrp2024}.

This tension is rarely resolved in a controlled manner, because comparisons that
motivate geometry priors typically change the architecture, the input format,
and the parameter count at once, confounding geometry with capacity and
modality. We instead isolate the prior with a single \emph{one-bit} intervention:
inside a geometry-aware channel-attention (GCA) block we toggle one additive
geometry bias on or off, holding the backbone, loss, optimizer, data, training
recipe, and parameter count (to within $80$ parameters) fixed. We embed this
toggle in the official DCASE~2024 SELD baseline~\cite{krause2024sde} and cross it
with two input modalities (MIC, FOA) and \emph{three} temporal backbones chosen
to span the locality--globality spectrum: a convolutional-recurrent network with
multi-head self-attention (CRNN with MHSA), a convolution--attention hybrid
(Conformer), and a Transformer-only temporal stack.

Our central finding is that the prior's effect on \emph{direction} is not binary
but \emph{graded by the backbone's temporal inductive bias}. As locality is
progressively relaxed---from a recurrent stack, through a convolution--attention
hybrid, to pure self-attention---the geometry effect moves from helpful, through
near-neutral, to harmful. A factorial ANOVA over the recurrent and Transformer
extremes confirms a significant architecture$\times$prior interaction
($F=4.62$, $p=0.039$ at $n=5$), while the Conformer consistently occupies the
middle of the ordering rather than violating it. The most robust effect is the
recurrent \emph{benefit} on Ambisonics: the prior improves localization by
$5.0\degsym$ in-domain (a trend, $p=0.082$) and replicates zero-shot on an
independent dataset at full significance ($\dz=-1.50$, $p=0.029$); the
Transformer harm reproduces directionally in- and cross-domain
($+5.6$--$5.7\degsym$). A second injection check, replacing the
channel-attention bias with an early convolutional feature bias, sharpens the
interpretation by limiting it: the Conformer remains near-neutral in some runs,
whereas Transformer contrasts move across rerun and checkpoint-selection paths,
and the microphone-array Transformer can even reverse sign relative to GCA. The
effect is therefore structured by architecture in the primary intervention, but
not invariant to injection site or training-selection details.
Orthogonally, the prior's effect on \emph{distance} follows a different rule: it
improves relative distance error for Ambisonics across all three backbones but not
the microphone array, so the architecture that governs direction is inert on
distance---a dissociation (the modality$\times$prior interaction a non-significant
trend at $n=5$) that underscores the prior's context-dependent value. Because all
effects are measured on real-world recorded acoustic scenes under matched compute,
the resulting ordering gives array-processing practitioners an actionable rule for
\emph{when} to inject array geometry, not merely \emph{whether} to.

\subsection{Contributions}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item We show that the effect of an array-geometry prior on SELD \emph{direction}
  is \textbf{graded by temporal inductive bias}: it helps a recurrent model,
  remains near-neutral to weakly helpful in a Conformer, and harms a Transformer,
  established by multi-seed paired ablation ($n=5$) on STARSS23 across six
  modality$\times$backbone cells, with a significant recurrent-versus-Transformer
  architecture$\times$prior interaction ($p=0.039$, seed-matched $p=0.029$) and a
  monotone trend across all three backbones (Sections~\ref{sec:main}--\ref{sec:anova}).
  \item We establish a \textbf{dissociation}: the temporal architecture that
  governs the direction effect is inert on \emph{distance}, whose prior benefit is
  independent of backbone and concentrated in Ambisonics by effect size (the
  modality$\times$prior interaction being a non-significant trend at $n=5$;
  Section~\ref{sec:synth}).
  \item We \textbf{anchor the pattern} with a zero-shot \emph{cross-dataset
  replication} on STARSS22: the full architecture ordering holds out-of-domain,
  and the recurrent Ambisonic benefit is significant there ($\dz=-1.50$,
  $p=0.029$) while the Transformer harm reproduces directionally
  (Section~\ref{sec:cross}). A controlled linear probe finds \emph{no} significant
  change in linearly decodable direction in any cell (Section~\ref{sec:probing}),
  indicating the effect is behavioural rather than a change in linear feature
  content---a negative result we report in full.
  \item We provide a \textbf{reproducible, statistically grounded protocol}
  (one-bit ablation, multi-seed paired tests, factorial interaction test, probing,
  and a second geometry-injection sensitivity check) and release the checkpoints
  needed to audit the claim, arguing that geometry priors should be ablated rather
  than assumed and that alternative injection sites should be treated as boundary
  conditions unless they are independently stabilized.
\end{enumerate}
